# What if You Could See Every Risk Before Your Team Steps Into the Field?

Real-time disaster intelligence is no longer optional. It's operational survival.

---

Picture this: You're planning a vaccination campaign across 15 districts. Your team is packed, supplies are loaded, and you're ready to deploy in 48 hours. Then you refresh your news feed and see reports of armed clashes in one of your target areas. Do you proceed? Delay? Reroute?

Now imagine another layer: there's a Category 4 cyclone 200km away, tracking toward three of your districts. Two health facilities in the path are yours. Your logistics coordinator is already on-route.

How do you make the call?

For most humanitarian organizations, the answer involves frantic calls, scattered spreadsheets, manual map checking across multiple browser tabs, and gut instinct. By the time you piece together the full picture, critical hours (sometimes days) have been lost.

There's a better way.

---

## The Problem: Too Many Data Sources, Too Little Time

Humanitarian operations exist in one of the most information-dense, time-sensitive environments imaginable. When disasters strike:

- GDACS publishes real-time alerts on earthquakes, cyclones, floods, and volcanic activity
- ACLED tracks armed conflicts and security incidents across 50+ countries
- Your organization maintains facilities data across dozens (maybe hundreds) of locations
- District boundaries define your operational zones, often stored in shapefiles that require GIS software to view
- Decision-makers need answers in minutes, not hours

But here's the reality: most organizations are stuck stitching these data sources together manually. A program manager in Nairobi doesn't have time to cross-reference GDACS alerts with facility spreadsheets while simultaneously checking ACLED for conflict patterns. The tools exist, but they're siloed, technical, and slow.

When every minute counts, your tools should keep up, not slow you down.

---

## What's Possible: Real-Time Operational Intelligence for Impact Workers

I built Aidstack Disasters to answer one question: What if field teams had the same real-time intelligence capabilities as Fortune 500 companies, but purpose-built for humanitarian work?

Here's what that looks like in practice.

### Scenario 1: Armed Clashes in Your Planned Operation Zone

You're deploying a malaria control campaign across rural districts. Before you leave:

1. Upload your district boundaries (shapefile) and facility locations (CSV/Excel)
2. View real-time ACLED security data overlaid on your operational map
3. Identify conflict patterns: Are clashes increasing? Concentrated in specific areas? Involving certain actors?
4. Get AI-powered viability assessment: Based on conflict proximity, severity, and trends, should you proceed, delay, or reroute?

Decision made in under 5 minutes. With data, not guesswork.

### Scenario 2: Multi-Hazard Impact Assessment

A 6.2 magnitude earthquake strikes in a region where you operate 30 health facilities. Simultaneously, there's a tropical cyclone 400km away, tracking inland.

With Aidstack Disasters:

1. Automatic impact detection: The system calculates which facilities fall within disaster impact zones (using GDACS CAP XML polygons for precision)
2. District-level risk mapping: See which operational districts are at highest risk, color-coded by severity
3. Facility prioritization: AI analyzes distance, disaster severity, and operational context to recommend which facilities need immediate attention
4. Logistics planning: Avoid disaster zones when planning supply routes; identify safe staging areas

Within 30 seconds of loading the page, you have a complete operational picture.

### Scenario 3: Real-Time "What If" Analysis

Your emergency response coordinator asks: "If we send a team to District X tomorrow, what risks do they face?"

Instead of pulling reports and calling security contacts, you:

1. Click on the target district on the map
2. View integrated risk assessment: Active disasters within 50km, recent security incidents, impacted facilities, population density
3. Ask the AI chatbot natural language questions:
   - "Are there any security incidents in the past 7 days?"
   - "Which facilities can we safely access?"
   - "What's the pattern of conflict events in this area?"
4. Generate a facility brief with AI recommendations in Word format for your field teams

From question to actionable brief: under 2 minutes.

## The Core Capabilities

Aidstack Disasters isn't just another mapping tool. It's a decision-support platform designed around how humanitarian teams actually work.

### 1. Integrated Data Streams
- GDACS: Live feed of earthquakes, cyclones, floods, tsunamis, volcanic activity, droughts, wildfires
- ACLED: Real-time conflict and security event data, filterable by country, region, event type, and date range
- Your facilities: Upload any CSV/Excel with lat/lon coordinates
- District boundaries: Import shapefiles to visualize operational zones with automatic coordinate system transformation

### 2. Automatic Impact Analysis
- Distance-based calculations: Which facilities are within disaster impact radii?
- District risk scoring: High, medium, low, or none (visualized with color-coded borders)
- Multi-hazard assessment: See conflicts and disasters simultaneously

### 3. AI-Powered Insights
- Interactive chatbot: Ask questions about your facilities in plain language
- Operation viability scoring: Should you proceed with a campaign based on security, disaster proximity, and operational factors? (Supports general operations, immunization, nutrition, shelter, WASH)
- Facility recommendations: AI-generated action plans for each at-risk location
- Comprehensive SitRep generation: One-click Word reports with analysis and recommendations

### 4. Designed for the Field
- No training required (intuitive visual interface)
- Works with your existing data (CSV, Excel, Shapefiles)
- No server-side data storage (your facility information stays in your browser)
- Export reports for offline sharing with stakeholders

What it does is multiply your capacity.
